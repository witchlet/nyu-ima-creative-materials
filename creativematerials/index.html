Hi! I'm Julie. I lied about my preferred name on my application — sorry about that. I've been writing music for 5 years and creating 3D art for slightly over two months. With my work, I aim to create engaging and overwhelming worlds of silliness, catharsis, and childlike wonder.

Selected Music

This section contains my best music. Many of my songs are sample-based. However, unless otherwise stated, they primarily feature my own original songwriting. In this assortment, the first four tracks have sampled vocals instead of my own. The samples used are listed in the description of each piece on SoundCloud, which is accessible by clicking on the tracks' titles. I decided to create a selection because I do not expect an admissions officer to have the time to listen to all of my music.

Headphones recommended over speakers for the best experience.
*HIGH VOLUME WARNING*

This song is a practice in absurdity and juxtaposition, particularly regarding dynamics and mood, that creates an engaging and fun atmosphere. Dreamy, minimal synths immediately interrupt the track's intense hyperactivity throughout its runtime. Through this opposition, in conjunction with the electronic soundscapes and post-ironic sampling of songs popular on TikTok, this piece aims to capture internet addiction and overstimulation.

Dubbed as "the worst song I've ever heard" by my junior year psychology teacher (I personally disagree), this song is a fast-paced, ever-morphing experience grounded only by its tempo and nearly constant volume. It reuporposes audio with diverse emotional palettes, such as Mitski's bitter "Washing Machine Heart" and Mei's panicked dialogue in Turning Red, into a hyperactive, spontaneous track as an act of grappling with and overcoming negative emotions.

With this piece, I aim to evoke an undeniable sense of fun and excitement. I do this by drawing from various nostalgic aesthetics, such as nightcore and retro bitcrushing. Parts of this song toy with tension and dissonance. It doesn't shy away from potentially being unpleasant; it invites it, and it uses that discomfort to make the rest of the song more gratifying.

This song mainly samples vocals from Ice Spice and acts as an homage toward her. Additionally, as the first track on my unfinished "pabucore" compilation, it serves to establish the aesthetics that I will maintain, develop, and subvert for the rest of the record — those being genre-bending experimentation, internet culture, non-sequitur musical passages, and high intensity.

This piece portrays the gradually shifting feelings of having a crush. To accomplish this, I experiment heavily with progressively melancholy vocals, dynamics, and sonic palettes. Through this, I aim to communicate cognitive dissonance, sorrow, and self-aware melodrama.

This track works to push the pop music trope of sad lyrics with happy instrumentation to its extreme. A repetitive and bouncy instrumental accompanies me yelling out "Did you even notice / that you led me on or / am I so pathetic that you didn't even have to try?". These lyrics are chipmunked, bitcrushed, and not at all understandable to exaggerate the obscured meaning that the aforementioned trope achieves. However, I also attempt to breathe new life into the practice: I hope the impossibly high, potentially annoying energy of this song wears the listener out in the same way that the lyrical content wears me out.

The oldest of the list, this song, along the album it is a part of, was created throughout the peak of quarantine. In it, I sing about feeling unworthy of even deserving a funeral, dissatisfaction with life, and gaslighting. Truthfully, the lyrics are messy and lack polish, but they perfectly mirror how I felt at the time. This song, beyond its message, also shows how I have evolved over time — both emotionally and musically.

Full Chronological Discography

I have released one album, three compilations, and multiple other songs.
Unless otherwise stated, the given dates indicate the release date.

web

Created: August 8, 2020 - October 23, 2021
Released: November 6, 2021

This album is an unpredictable mess of teenage angst, with lyrics intentionally obfuscated out of embarrassment.

retsycore

October 22, 2021 - January 8, 2022

This compilation is the first in the red panda trilogy. It features playful sampling and the exploration of various genres.

January 26, 2022

This piece features little original songwriting; it is less of an original song and more of a mashup. It was made for the community compilation "SNARE SOCIETY VOL. 1", in which artists had 24 hours to create a song. With the mashup, I aimed to explore the parallels between the genres of shoegaze and HexD.

meicore

March 19, 2022 - October 23, 2022

This is the second compilation in the red panda trilogy. It builds upon the established tools of "retsycore" by introducing abstract storytelling, utilizing more genres, and furthering the contrast of extremes.

July 25, 2022

This track is an ode to the animated series "Dead End: Paranormal Park". All of the samples are from the musical episode "The Phantom of the Theme Park". With the song, I (ironically) experiment with less experimentation.

starstarstar

August 28, 2022 - October 9, 2022

This mini-compilation continues the HexD style of ✰ CERES & CALYPSO IN THE DEEP CLUB ✰ with a much more EDM-centric approach. The lyrics explore the feelings of having an unrequited crush through the lenses of children's cartoons and magical bugs.

November 22, 2022

This non-album single is unfortunately about that same crush. It is my most unfiltered and personal song.

Created: December 11, 2022 - December 15, 2022
Released: January 3, 2023

This was my song for the "SNARE SOCIETY VOL. 4", which features Rico Nasty as the main sample. However, it also features my own vocals. It toys with tempo, genre, and mood.

pabucore

January 1, 2023 - ?? [unfinished]

This is what exists of the third compilation in the red panda trilogy. With this LP, I want to further experiment with the practices of "retsycore" and "meicore" and push them to their limits.
